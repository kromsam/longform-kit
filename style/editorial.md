# Editorial Style

This is Longform Kit's starter house style. A downstream project owns this
file and should adapt it to its author, discipline, and publisher. It is the
authority for prose; Vale and Harper provide useful evidence but do not
override editorial judgement.

## Language

- Use Oxford English spelling. Prefer forms such as *organize* and
  *organization*, but *analyse* and *paralyse*, alongside *theatre*, *centre*,
  *colour*, and *behaviour*.
- Preserve the spelling and punctuation of quotations, titles, proper names,
  and cited editions. Mark an original anomaly only when readers genuinely
  need the clarification.

## Punctuation and typography

- Use the serial comma in a list of three or more items.
- Use logical punctuation: place commas and full stops outside a closing
  quotation mark unless they belong to the quoted material.
- Use typographic single quotation marks (‘…’) at the primary level and double
  quotation marks (“…”) for a quotation within a quotation.
- Use a spaced en rule for a parenthetical break – like this – and a closed en
  rule for ranges, such as 1999–2002 and pp. 168–69.
- Write dates as day, full month, and year without commas: 9 August 2025.

## Citations and notes

- Keep citations semantic in the source and let Pandoc and the selected CSL
  style determine their presentation. Do not hand-format a bibliography.
- Place a Markdown footnote marker immediately after the relevant punctuation.
- Use citation signals without a colon (*See @key*; *See also @key*) and avoid
  *op. cit.* and *loc. cit.*

Record project-specific departures here, and align lint configuration only
when a rule can be checked without flattening context, voice, or meaning.
