# Williams And Bizup Revision Checklist

Use these principles diagnostically. They are not mechanical rules, and they do
not override disciplinary precision or the project's editorial style.

## Sentences

- Make the central characters of an account easy to locate.
- Express their consequential actions in verbs where possible.
- Diagnose dense nominalizations; retain them when they name a necessary
  concept, but unpack chains that hide agency or sequence.
- Keep short opening context before longer, unfamiliar, or technically complex
  material.
- Reserve sentence endings for information that should carry emphasis or lead
  into the next sentence.
- Prefer explicit logical relations when a reader would otherwise have to infer
  the connection.
- Preserve productive qualification. Concision is not the removal of nuance.

## Paragraphs

- Make the paragraph's opening establish a stable topic or point of departure.
- Track the sequence of grammatical subjects. Unmotivated topic changes often
  reveal weak cohesion.
- Distinguish local evidence from the claim it supports.
- End with the paragraph's consequence, complication, or transition rather than
  a loose detail.
- Split a paragraph only when it performs more than one conceptual task.

## Whole-Passage Review

- Confirm that key terms retain the same meaning unless a shift is intentional.
- Check that pronouns and demonstratives have unambiguous referents.
- Preserve the scope of negatives, modal verbs, and hedges.
- Read citations with their sentences so that a revision does not change which
  source supports which claim.
- Read the revision aloud for rhythm, excessive coordination, and accidental
  repetition.
- Report conceptual gaps rather than silently supplying new argument or
  evidence.
